A Guide to Contracting

A basic guide to weighing up the options and getting up and running.

It’s great that you’re considering becoming a contractor and I know that it’s hard to find the information you need to convince you to take the plunge or not.

This post isn’t intended to sway you in either direction but rather reflect on some of my experiences since starting out.

Why I Chose Contracting

The biggest appeal for me was the idea that I would gain more exposure to different technologies and approaches in a relatively short amount of time. I tend to think of contracting as the only real way of staying ahead of new technology.

The more obvious benefits are:

  • Larger income
  • Flexible working hours and holiday
  • Able to work for multiple clients

Note: Companies aren’t obliged to make a contractual provision for sick pay and if you’re working for a company that doesn’t run it’s own sick pay scheme you’re stuck with ‘Statutory Sick Pay (SSP)’ which pays £87.55 a week.

Umbrella or Limited

I chose both. Initially I went with an umbrella to hold the fort while I carefully investigated the option of a limited company. Providing that the umbrella doesn’t stipulate fees for you leaving and nonsense like that you can use it for as long as you need. I would personally recommend Accessa.

Find an Accountant That Works for You

I highly recommend FreeAgent and finding an accountant who will work with you using it.
