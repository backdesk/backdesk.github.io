Mobile First, Does it Work?

I’ve often wondered if a ‘Mobile First’ approach is more of an ideal than a concrete strategy.

As we know mobile is (most likely) the forseeable future of the web and the pace of this revolution is rapidly driving an urgency for companies to have a strategy in place. A popular trend in strategy leans towards the concept of ‘Mobile First’.

The intention of this post is to share some of the conclusions I arrived at when studying the many definitons of ‘Mobile First’.

Prototypes vs. Flat Designs

We had relatively few problems to solve when I first became a web developer. Designs were handed to me in the form of a Photoshop file which I did my utmost to make pixel perfect using HTML/CSS. There wasn’t a real requirement for responsive behavior.

These days I engage a lot more with designers and at an earlier stage as well. In the past it might of been that design was a production phase where upon its completion you would receive assets to work with. That doesn’t quite cut the mustard any more.

I’ve learned that it’s important to get involved with the concept at an early stage and from there start working towards building a prototype.

I guess you could argue that the mobile aspect of the design is the simplest and for that reason - this is where we should start.

Context Branching vs. Context Adaptation

I tend to think of audiences as contexts under which an application runs.

In my opinion a good example of this failure is Windows 8. But that’s probably a topic best left for another post!

Copy

How you write and revise copy should also be a consideration in an adaptive solution. A moderately large block of copy might look great on the higher resolutions but lengthly and overkill on mobile.
